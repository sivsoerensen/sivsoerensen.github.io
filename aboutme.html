About me

I'm Siv—originally from a small town on the west coast of Denmark. Since 2016 I've been living in Copenhagen where I studied to become an Engineer at The Technical University of Denmark (DTU). Fast forward eight years—a degree and a PhD later—and I'm still here.

I enjoy living with my boyfriend north of Copenhagen surrounded by forest and the sea, where meeting my daily step count or going for a dip is easy. Nature and health means a lot to me and I try to spend some time outdoors every day and enjoy cooking (and eating!) homemade meals from fresh ingredients. Vegetables and seafood are my favorites and I have enjoyed a predominantly pescetarian diet for many years now.

Breakfast is my 'bread and butter' (quite literally) and I love baking my own sourdough— or visit one of my favorite bakeries on the weekend. Matcha and Coffee are also big favorites! I am however very bad with caffeine and hope one day to see decaf coffee being as common in cafes in northen Europe as it is in the south.

I've always been a very creative and curious person and I've always obsessively enjoyed learning and making things; LEGOs as a kid, sewing without patterns as a teen (very frustrating process, why I do it to myself I don't know), knitting, drawing, and as of recently writing. I think that's why I wanted to become an engineer—to be able to understand how things work, recoginze patterns and apply that knowledge in new and creative ways.

Reading has been an escape and addiction of mine since I churned through all of the Harry Potter books in the the spring of fourth grade. Since then I've always come back to reading even though I at times have had to force myself on a detox to stay focused on studying for exams or other much less thrilling tasks.

I started my career thinking that Mechanical Engineering was the perfect balance between math and creating things, but quickly realized that math and logic is what I am actually passionate about—also my Physics inituion (or rather lack thereof) is simply not helping. I therefore purused a Master's degree in Mathematical Computation and Modelling where I focused mainly on Operations Research (optimization), algorithms, data structures, and ML.

Finding my 'niche' quite late in my studies I felt that I wasn't quite done learning yet and decided to continue on to a PhD in Operations Research.
